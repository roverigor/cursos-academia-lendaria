# Why You Can't Hire

**Author:** Naval Ravikant
**Date:** December 13, 2011
**Source:** startupboy.com (now at nav.al)
**URL:** https://nav.al/why-you-cant-hire

---

## Article Content

The problem with hiring isn't a talent shortage, but a surplus of founders.

### Key Points

Naval argues that the problem with hiring isn't a talent shortage, but a surplus of founders. He highlights several key observations:

1. **Starting a company is now much cheaper:**
   - "Desks are free, hosting is free, marketing is online, and company setup is cheap"

2. **Early funding is relatively accessible:**
   - First $25K can come from incubators
   - Next $100K from investors following incubators

3. **Hiring challenges are most acute for pre-traction companies:**
   - Established companies like Yammer, Square, and Twitter can hire more easily
   - Pre-traction startups struggle to attract talent

4. **Equity expectations need to change:**
   - Traditional cap tables are outdated
   - Early engineers should be treated more like "late founders"
   - Equity allocation needs to be more competitive

## Core Insight

> "There isn't a shortage of developers and designers. There's a _surplus of founders_."

The article suggests that startups must "Close the equity gap" to make hiring easier, recognizing that early technical talent is critical to a company's success.
