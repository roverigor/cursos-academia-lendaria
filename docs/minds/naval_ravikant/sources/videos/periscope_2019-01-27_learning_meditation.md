# Naval Ravikant - Periscope Session: January 27, 2019
**Topic:** Learning, Meditation, Work & Life Philosophy
**Source:** Podcast Notes compilation
**Duration:** ~1 hour
**Value Score:** 9/10
**Note:** "The long awaited return" - first session after several months

## Overview
Naval's return to Periscope after a hiatus. Focuses on becoming a perpetual learner, meditation practice, work philosophy, and future technology trends.

## Key Themes

### 1. Learning and Personal Development
- Become a "perpetual learner"
- Read foundational books, especially originals and classics
- Focus on building high-quality knowledge foundations
- "The most important skill for getting rich – becoming a perpetual learner"
- Quality of knowledge base matters more than quantity

### 2. Meditation Practice
- Meditation is "the art of doing nothing"
- Sit for 1 hour daily, with no effort
- Goal is to reach "mental inbox zero"
- "You're actually in deep meditation all the time. It's just covered up by all the noise"
- Meditation reveals what's already there

### 3. Work and Wealth Philosophy
- Seek higher leverage and specific knowledge
- "When you do things for their own sake, that's when you create your best work"
- Value freedom over money
- "I value freedom above anything else"
- Intrinsic motivation produces best results

### 4. Technology and Future Trends
- Internet is "the real country"
- Emerging "high-end gig economy"
- Coding is a "superpower edge" in any profession
- "You cannot go wrong having coding knowledge"
- Technology enables sovereignty

### 5. Life Philosophy
- Nothing ultimately matters in the long term
- Do things for their own sake
- "Everything goes to 0 eventually"
- Nihilism as liberation, not depression
- "Fair doesn't exist in the world"
