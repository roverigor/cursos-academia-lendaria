## Business Trajectory

### Early Years (1948-1959)

**Age 12 (1948)**: Started working at father's Doceria Pão de Açúcar

**1959**: Graduated from FGV (Fundação Getulio Vargas) in Business Administration

**April 1959**: Partnered with father Valentim to create **first Pão de Açúcar supermarket** on Brigadeiro Luiz Antônio Avenue, São Paulo

### Expansion Era (1960s-1970s)

**1968**: Pão de Açúcar reached **40 supermarkets** and **1,642 employees**. Abilio established himself as prominent young executive.

**1971**: Founded **Jumbo**, Brazil's first hypermarket, in Santo André (inspired by Carrefour model)

**1979-1989**: Served on **Monetary National Council** (Conselho Monetário Nacional)

### Crisis Period (1980s-1990s)

**Three Breaking Points** (Abilio's framework for life-defining crises):

1. **Family Business Conflict** (1980s)
   Internal family disputes over company direction

2. **Kidnapping** (December 11, 1989)
   7 days in captivity (detailed below)

3. **Near-Bankruptcy** (1990)
   Pão de Açúcar almost disappeared, on brink of financial collapse

### Consolidation & Sale (2000s)

**2004**: Published first book **"Caminhos e Escolhas"** (220,000+ copies sold)

**2005**: Sold large stake to French company **Casino Group** for estimated **$860 million**, stepped down as CEO but remained as Chairman

**September 2013**: After **54 years**, left Grupo Pão de Açúcar

### Reinvention Era (2010s-2020s)

**April 2013**: Invited by Tarpon investment firm, assumed presidency of **BRF Board of Administration** (first time in industrial segment)

**2014-2015**: Through Península Participações, acquired **10% of Carrefour Brazil** shares. By March 2016, became **3rd largest global shareholder** of Carrefour chain.

**2010**: Created **"Liderança 360º" (Leadership 360°)** specialization course at **FGV**

**2014/2016**: Published second book **"Novos Caminhos, Novas Escolhas"** (New Paths, New Choices) with journalist Sergio Malbergier

**2022-2024**: Hosted **"Caminhos com Abilio Diniz"** (Paths with Abilio Diniz) on **CNN Brasil**, interviewing business executives

## Personal Philosophy & Transformation

### The Three Breaking Points

Abilio identified **three critical moments** that forced profound personal transformation:

1. **Family Conflicts** - Business disagreements, power struggles
2. **1989 Kidnapping** - 7 days captivity, PTSD, existential crisis
3. **1990 Near-Bankruptcy** - Company survival threat, financial crisis

**Core Principle**:
> "In life we grow more in suffering and sadness than in joy. When you go through this, you either grow or perish, either sink or grow."

### Transformation Journey

**Before Crises**: Authoritarian, stressed, work-obsessed, imbalanced

**After Transformation**: Focus on balance, quality of life, spirituality, physical wellness, teaching/mentoring

**Catalysts**:
- Therapy and psychological work (post-kidnapping)
- Self-knowledge and introspection
- Spirituality and Catholic faith
- Physical fitness and sports
- Family prioritization

## Books Published

### 1. Caminhos e Escolhas (2004)

**Full Title**: "Abilio Diniz: Caminhos e Escolhas - O Equilíbrio para uma Vida Mais Feliz" (Paths and Choices - Balance for a Happier Life)

**Publisher**: Elsevier (January 2004)

**Full Title**: "Novos Caminhos, Novas Escolhas: Gestão, Liderança, Motivação, Equilíbrio, Longevidade e Fé" (New Paths, New Choices: Management, Leadership, Motivation, Balance, Longevity and Faith)

**Co-Author**: Sergio Malbergier (journalist)
**Publisher**: Editora Sextante

**Content**: Written 10 years after first book, reflecting on "best years of his life" and significant learning/maturation. Focus on management, leadership, motivation, balance, longevity, faith.

**Initial Plan**: Abilio was certain he'd never write another book, but experienced what he considered his best years, leading to this second volume.
