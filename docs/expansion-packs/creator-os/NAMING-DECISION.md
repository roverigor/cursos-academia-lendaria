## Executive Summary

After analyzing product scope (content creation + marketing + education), we have decided to name this expansion pack **CreatorOS** (or **AIOS CreatorOS** for brand clarity).

**Rationale:**
1. ✅ **Scope-perfect:** "Creator" encompasses content creators, marketers, educators, product builders
2. ✅ **Positioning:** "OS" positions as a system (not just a tool)
3. ✅ **Memorable:** Unique, aspirational, easy to communicate
4. ✅ **Scalable:** Accommodates future features (courses, funnels, A/B testing, attribution)
5. ✅ **Ecosystem fit:** Complements MMOS (personality cloning) and InnerLens (psychometrics)

## Trademark & Legal Considerations

### Existing "CreatorOS" Projects

**Research Findings:**
- ✅ Other projects use "CreatorOS" name
- ✅ Our context: Expansion pack (not standalone product)
- ✅ Open source (non-commercial initially)

### Differentiation Strategy

**1. Compound Branding**
```
AIOS CreatorOS
└── "CreatorOS" is the expansion pack name
└── "AIOS" is the parent framework
└── Combined mark: "AIOS CreatorOS"
```

**2. Legal Disclaimer (Optional)**
```
"CreatorOS may be a trademark of other entities.
AIOS CreatorOS is an independent open-source project
under the AIOS-FULLSTACK framework."
```

**3. Logo Differentiation**
- Use AIOS icon + CreatorOS wordmark
- Distinct visual identity
- Always paired with "by AIOS-FULLSTACK"

**4. URL/Domain Strategy**
- Primary: `aios-creator-os` (GitHub repo)
- Docs: `docs.aios.dev/creator-os`
- NOT: `creatoros.com` (avoid domain conflict)

### Risk Assessment

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-------------|--------|------------|
| Trademark conflict | Low | Medium | Use compound mark "AIOS CreatorOS" |
| Brand confusion | Low | Low | Clear branding + subtitle |
| Legal challenge | Very Low | Low | Open source, non-commercial, expansion pack context |

**Conclusion:** **Low risk.** Proceed with "CreatorOS" name using compound branding strategy.

## FAQ: Naming Decision

**Q: Why "OS" and not "Studio"?**
A: "OS" positions as a **system** (comprehensive, foundational) vs "Studio" which sounds like a **tool** (single-purpose). We want users to think "this is my operating system for creation" not "this is one tool among many."

**Q: What about trademark conflicts with other "CreatorOS" projects?**
A: We use compound branding ("AIOS CreatorOS") and operate as an open-source expansion pack, not a standalone commercial product. This differentiates us legally and contextually.

**Q: Why not "Content-Marketing-Studio" (more descriptive)?**
A: Too long, too specific. "Creator" is broader (includes educators, product builders, not just marketers). "OS" is stronger positioning than "Studio."

**Q: Does "CreatorOS" limit us to creator economy only?**
A: No. "Creator" is universal: content creators, course creators, campaign creators, product creators. It's aspirational and inclusive.

**Q: What if we add features outside "creation" (e.g., analytics)?**
A: "OS" covers everything. An operating system includes creation tools, analytics, management, automation—all fits under CreatorOS umbrella.
