### PARTE 1: DIALECTICAL LENS (1:30 - 7:00)

**[CENA: Screencast - NotebookLM]**

#### O que é:

A Lente Dialética força o NotebookLM a analisar tensões e contradições internas no material.

**Por que funciona:** Todo documento tem premissas não-ditas e potenciais contradições. Essa técnica revela o que está "entre as linhas".

---

#### Prompt Base:

```
Apply a dialectical analysis to these sources:

1. THESIS: What is the main argument or position presented?

2. ANTITHESIS: What counter-arguments or tensions exist within
   the material itself? What would critics say?

3. SYNTHESIS: How can these opposing views be reconciled?
   What nuanced conclusion emerges?

4. HIDDEN ASSUMPTIONS: What does the author assume without stating?

5. INTERNAL CONTRADICTIONS: Where does the argument contradict
   itself or other sources?

Be specific. Quote directly from sources when identifying tensions.
```

**[DEMONSTRAR - executar prompt]**

---

#### Exemplo Prático:

**Contexto:** Documento sobre "crescimento a qualquer custo" de uma startup

**Output esperado:**
```
THESIS:
O documento defende crescimento acelerado como prioridade #1...

ANTITHESIS:
Porém, na página 12, o próprio autor menciona que "queimaram
caixa rápido demais", contradizendo a tese principal...

SÍNTESE:
A conclusão mais nuançada é: crescimento é importante, mas
precisa ser sustentável. O documento não resolve essa tensão...

HIDDEN ASSUMPTIONS:
- Assume que venture capital estará sempre disponível
- Assume que mercado é winner-takes-all
- Não considera modelo bootstrapped como válido
```

---

#### Variações:

```
PARA DOCUMENTOS TÉCNICOS:
"Apply dialectical analysis focusing on technical trade-offs
and engineering decisions that have both benefits and costs"

PARA DOCUMENTOS DE NEGÓCIO:
"Analyze the strategic tensions between short-term gains
and long-term sustainability in this material"

PARA PESQUISA ACADÊMICA:
"Identify methodological tensions and limitations the
authors acknowledge vs. those they don't address"
```

### PARTE 2: DISILLUSIONMENT FILTER (7:00 - 12:30)

**[CENA: Screencast - NotebookLM]**

#### O que é:

O Filtro de Desilusão remove o "hype" e analisa com ceticismo saudável. Força análise crítica.

**Por que funciona:** A maioria dos documentos (especialmente de marketing, vendas, pitch decks) são otimistas demais. Essa técnica equilibra.

---

#### Prompt Base:

```
Apply a "Disillusionment Filter" to these sources:

1. CLAIMS vs EVIDENCE
   - What bold claims are made?
   - What evidence actually supports them?
   - Rate each claim: Strong/Moderate/Weak evidence

2. WHAT'S MISSING
   - What information would a skeptic want?
   - What data is conspicuously absent?
   - What questions go unanswered?

3. BEST CASE vs REALISTIC CASE
   - What's the optimistic scenario presented?
   - What's a more realistic outcome?
   - What could go wrong that isn't mentioned?

4. INCENTIVE ANALYSIS
   - Who wrote this and why?
   - What do they gain if you believe them?
   - How might this bias the content?

Be ruthlessly objective. I want the uncomfortable truths.
```

**[DEMONSTRAR - executar prompt]**

---

#### Exemplo Prático:

**Contexto:** Pitch deck de startup pedindo investimento

**Output esperado:**
```
CLAIMS vs EVIDENCE:

Claim: "Market size of $50B by 2025"
Evidence: Cites one analyst report from 2021
Rating: WEAK - Single source, outdated, TAM vs SAM unclear

Claim: "10x better than competitors"
Evidence: Internal benchmark, methodology not disclosed
Rating: WEAK - No third-party validation

WHAT'S MISSING:
- Customer retention/churn data (red flag)
- Unit economics breakdown
- Competitor response strategy
- Why previous fundraise details hidden

REALISTIC CASE:
Best case: $10M ARR in 3 years
Presented: $50M ARR in 3 years
Gap: 5x overestimate based on comparable companies

INCENTIVE ANALYSIS:
Writers are founders seeking funding. Strong incentive
to present optimistic scenario. No mention of risks
or previous failures.
```

---

#### Variações:

```
PARA RELATÓRIOS DE MERCADO:
"Apply skeptical analysis to the market projections.
Who paid for this research? What methodology biases exist?"

PARA ESTUDOS DE CASO:
"Strip away the success narrative. What actually went wrong?
What luck was involved? Would this replicate?"

PARA CONTEÚDO DE THOUGHT LEADERSHIP:
"Separate genuine insight from self-promotion.
What's actually new vs. repackaged conventional wisdom?"
```

### PARTE 3: TÉCNICA DA ANTÍTESE (12:30 - 17:00)

**[CENA: Screencast - NotebookLM]**

#### O que é:

A Antítese força o NotebookLM a construir o argumento contrário mais forte possível.

**Por que funciona:** Entender a oposição fortalece sua própria posição. É o "red team" do pensamento crítico.

---

#### Prompt Base:

```
Construct the strongest possible counter-argument to the
main thesis in these sources:

1. STEELMAN THE OPPOSITION
   - What's the best argument AGAINST the main position?
   - Assume a smart, informed critic. What would they say?
   - Use evidence and logic, not just emotion

2. VULNERABILITIES
   - Where is the original argument weakest?
   - What evidence would disprove the thesis?
   - What scenarios would make it fail?

3. ALTERNATIVE EXPLANATIONS
   - How else could the same data be interpreted?
   - What other conclusions are possible?
   - What does the author dismiss too quickly?

4. IF I HAD TO ARGUE AGAINST THIS...
   - Build a 3-point rebuttal as if debating
   - Make it persuasive and evidence-based

The goal is not to destroy the argument, but to understand
its limits and where it could be strengthened.
```

**[DEMONSTRAR - executar prompt]**

---

#### Exemplo Prático:

**Contexto:** Artigo defendendo trabalho remoto 100%

**Output esperado:**
```
STEELMAN OPPOSITION:

A critic would argue:
1. Innovation suffers: Major breakthroughs historically happen
   through serendipitous in-person interactions (Bell Labs,
   Xerox PARC). The article ignores this research.

2. Junior employee development: The article assumes all workers
   are senior. Juniors learn through osmosis and informal
   mentoring that's harder to replicate remotely.

3. Company culture erosion: The article cites productivity
   metrics but ignores long-term cultural cohesion...

VULNERABILITIES:
- All cited studies are from 2020-2021 (pandemic unusual period)
- Selection bias: Companies that went remote were already
  tech-forward and high-performing
- No data beyond 2 years of remote work

ALTERNATIVE EXPLANATION:
The productivity gains could be explained by:
- Elimination of commute stress (temporary effect)
- Novelty effect (wears off)
- Employees working MORE hours, not more efficiently
```

---

#### Variações:

```
PARA DECISÕES DE NEGÓCIO:
"I'm about to make this decision. Play devil's advocate.
Why might this be wrong? What am I missing?"

PARA PROPOSTAS:
"Before I submit this proposal, attack it. What objections
will stakeholders have? How do I preempt them?"

PARA ESTRATÉGIA:
"Assume our competitor is smart. How would they counter
this strategy? What would a hostile analyst say?"
```

### PARTE 4: COMBINANDO AS TÉCNICAS (17:00 - 19:00)

**[CENA: Screencast - prompt combinado]**

O poder real vem de combinar:

```
MEGA-PROMPT: COMPREHENSIVE CRITICAL ANALYSIS

Analyze these sources using three lenses:

LENS 1: DIALECTICAL
- What are the internal tensions and contradictions?
- What synthesis emerges from opposing elements?

LENS 2: DISILLUSIONMENT
- Strip away hype. What's the evidence-based reality?
- What's missing that should be here?

LENS 3: ANTITHESIS
- What's the strongest counter-argument?
- Where could this be completely wrong?

FINAL SYNTHESIS:
After applying all three lenses, what is your most nuanced,
balanced assessment of this material?

What should a decision-maker actually believe?
```
