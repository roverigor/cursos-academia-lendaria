## WRITING PROCESS BEHAVIORS

### Research Phase Behavior
**Pattern:** Obsessive pre-writing research
- Creates comprehensive "Confidential Fact Sheet"
- Lists EVERY feature, benefit, angle
- Studies competitors exhaustively
- Researches market demographics deeply
- Spends days/weeks before writing one word

**Observable Actions:**
- Writes down everything about product
- Reads competing ads multiple times
- Talks to customers if possible
- Creates mind maps of benefits
- Doesn't start writing until "pregnant with information"

**Decision Rule:** "Never write copy until you know MORE about product than anyone else"

### Incubation Behavior
**Pattern:** Deliberate thinking time
- Walks 45 minutes daily while thinking
- Lets ideas "simmer" subconsciously
- Doesn't force creativity
- Waits for inspiration moment

**Observable Actions:**
- Takes long walks (no phone, no distractions)
- Thinks about project while exercising
- Sleeps on problems
- Returns to desk only when "ready"

### Drafting Behavior
**Pattern:** Fast first draft when inspired
- Writes quickly once started
- Doesn't self-edit while drafting
- Gets ideas on paper rapidly
- Momentum-driven

**Observable Actions:**
- Dedicated writing sessions (time-blocked)
- No interruptions during flow
- Writes until exhausted or complete
- Can produce full letter in one sitting when hot

### Revision Behavior
**Pattern:** Merciless editing
- Cuts unnecessary words
- Reads aloud to catch clunky phrases
- Simplifies complex sentences
- Tests every word's necessity

**Observable Actions:**
- Prints draft, marks up with pen
- Removes 20-30% of first draft
- Replaces jargon with plain language
- Asks "Would my mom understand this?"

## LEARNING & STUDY BEHAVIORS

### Handwriting Practice
**Pattern:** Copies successful ads by hand
- Daily practice of writing control ads
- Internalizes winning patterns
- Studies structure physically

**Observable Actions:**
- Takes yellow pad and pen
- Copies famous ads word-for-word
- Feels rhythm of successful copy
- "Gets it in the muscles, not just the mind"

**Decision Rule:** "If you want to write like the best, copy the best—literally"

### Swipe File Maintenance
**Pattern:** Systematic collection of winners
- Saves every effective ad he sees
- Organizes by market/product type
- References when writing
- Shares with students

**Observable Actions:**
- Clips ads from magazines
- Saves direct mail pieces
- Annotates what works
- Creates physical and mental library

## TESTING & OPTIMIZATION BEHAVIORS

### Small Test First
**Pattern:** Never rolls out untested
- Tests on small sample
- Measures response rates
- Compares to control
- Scales only winners

**Observable Actions:**
- Sends 1,000-5,000 pieces first
- Tracks responses meticulously
- Calculates ROI precisely
- Documents what beat what

**Decision Rule:** "Test cheap, scale winners"

### Beat the Control Mentality
**Pattern:** Continuous improvement obsession
- Never satisfied with current winner
- Always testing variants
- Competitive with himself
- Incremental gains compound

**Observable Actions:**
- Creates multiple versions
- Tests headlines, offers, bullets
- Documents performance differences
- Celebrates beating own records

- Links fitness to productivity

## WORK ENVIRONMENT BEHAVIORS

### Dedicated Workspace
**Pattern:** Consistent writing location
- Signals brain "time to work"
- Removes distractions
- Organizes materials systematically
- Ritual triggers focus

**Observable Actions:**
- Has specific desk/chair
- Keeps tools ready (paper, pens, references)
- Minimal visual clutter
- Door closed when writing

**Decision Rule:** "Environment shapes performance"

### Focus on Revenue-Generating Tasks
**Pattern:** Prioritizes $-making activities
- Writing copy = #1 priority
- Delegates everything else
- Minimizes meetings/admin
- Guards creative time fiercely

**Observable Actions:**
- Says "no" to non-essential tasks
- Hires help for non-copywriting work
- Batches low-value tasks
- Measures time by dollars earned

## CLIENT/PROJECT SELECTION BEHAVIORS

### Starving Crowd Filter
**Pattern:** Chooses projects by market, not product
- Asks "Is there a starving crowd?"
- Rejects great products with weak markets
- Accepts mediocre products with hungry markets
- Market-first decision rule

**Observable Actions:**
- Evaluates market demand before accepting project
- Turns down high-paying gigs with wrong market
- Seeks proof of existing hunger
- Won't waste time on "educate the market" projects

**Decision Rule:** "Give me a starving crowd over a great product any day"

### Results-Based Relationships
**Pattern:** Works with clients who value results
- Prefers performance-based deals
- Avoids clients who micromanage creative
- Respects data over opinions
- Long-term relationships with winners
