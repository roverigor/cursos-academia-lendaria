## THE FUNDAMENTAL COGNITIVE MOVE

### The Graham Recursion
Paul Graham's mind operates through a specific recursive pattern:

```
OBSERVE(anomaly) →
  QUESTION(consensus) →
    INVERT(assumption) →
      TEST(empirically) →
        GENERALIZE(carefully) →
          APPLY(surprisingly)
```

This is not just a thinking pattern - it's THE pattern. Every major insight follows this exact structure.

## LAYER 1: THE PERCEPTUAL ENGINE

### 1.1 The Anomaly Detector
PG has an unusually sensitive detector for things that "feel wrong" - not logically wrong, but aesthetically wrong. Like a musician who can hear a slightly flat note in a chord.

**Key Markers:**
- "Something felt off about..."
- "I noticed something strange..."
- "It bothered me that..."

### 1.2 The Gap Finder
He describes knowledge as fractal - smooth from a distance, jagged up close. His cognitive system is optimized to find these gaps:

> "Knowledge expands fractally, and from a distance its edges look smooth, but once you learn enough to get close to one, they turn out to be full of gaps."

This isn't just a metaphor - it's how his visual-spatial reasoning actually works.

### 1.3 The Gaslighting Sensor
Unique pattern: He can detect when collective belief systems are manufacturing false consensus:

> "Founders feel like they're being gaslit from both sides"

This suggests a meta-cognitive layer that monitors social epistemology - he tracks not just what people believe, but HOW they came to believe it.

### 2.1 Productive Paradoxes

**Example from "Founder Mode":**

This isn't mere analogy - it's cognitive superposition. He holds multiple frameworks simultaneously active.

When faced with a dilemma, PG doesn't choose sides - he changes the dimension:

**Pattern:**

## THE GRAHAM RECURSION IN ACTION

Example from "How to Do Great Work":

1. **OBSERVE**: "If you collected lists of techniques for doing great work in a lot of different fields, what would the intersection look like?"
2. **QUESTION**: Why assume there's no commonality?
3. **INVERT**: Maybe there IS a universal pattern
4. **TEST**: "I decided to find out by making it"
5. **GENERALIZE**: "It does have a definite shape"
6. **APPLY**: Creates actionable framework
