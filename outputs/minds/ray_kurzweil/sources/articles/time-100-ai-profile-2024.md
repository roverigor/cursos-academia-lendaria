## Profile Summary

Ray Kurzweil is recognized as an AI Visionary and Principal Researcher at Google, known for his remarkably prescient predictions about technological advancement.

## Predictive Achievements

### Historical Predictions (Validated)
- **1990**: Correctly predicted AI would beat chess champions before 2000
- **1990s**: Forecast mobile devices connected to global networks in the following decade
- **1999**: Projected computers would match human intelligence by 2029

## Philosophical Perspective

Kurzweil believes in the transformative potential of AI, particularly in medical advances. He envisions a **"singularity" around 2045** where:
> "Humans merge with AI via brain-to-computer interfaces to become 'much smarter'."

## Longevity Focus

### Personal Protocol
- Takes **80 pills daily** to maximize his chances of living through technological breakthroughs
- Believes scientific progress will eventually allow humans to **"live forever"**

### Life Extension Theory
- **For every year you age, scientific advancements give back approximately four months**
- Net result: aging slows down as technology accelerates
- Goal: Reach "longevity escape velocity" where tech adds more years than aging removes

## Expert Recognition

### Bill Gates Endorsement
> "The best person I know at predicting the future of artificial intelligence"

### Influence on AI Leaders
- **Inspired Shane Legg** - Co-founder of DeepMind
- **Inspired Jan Leike** - Leading AI researcher at Anthropic

## Current Stance on AI

Kurzweil remains optimistic about AI development:
> "You can certainly talk about problems that come with intelligence, but generally people want to be smarter."
