## STRATEGIC FRAMEWORK: LINCHPIN THINKING

### Core Philosophy
**"Qual é O elemento que, se resolvido, destrava todo o sistema?"**

Most businesses have 100 problems. You focus on THE ONE that changes everything.

### Linchpin Identification Process

**Step 1: Map the System**
What are all components?
- Funil (acquisition)
- Produto (delivery)
- Atendimento (retention)
- Monetização (pricing)
- Operação (execution)

**Step 2: Identify the Bottleneck**
Which ONE point, if solved, changes everything?
- NOT "most important" → THE LINCHPIN (única trava)
- Usually not obvious (hidden in system dynamics)

**Step 3: Focus Obsessively**
- Ignore secondary optimizations
- 80% resources on linchpin
- 20% on maintenance

**Example:**
- Business: AI education for non-tech people
- Linchpin: Translation of complexity (if fails, everything fails)
- NOT: Marketing, pricing, platform
- IS: Can you make non-tech person UNDERSTAND? (linchpin)

## THREE-QUESTION FILTER (Strategic Decisions)

Every major business decision passes through:

### Question 1: Vai gerar impacto real?
Not "revenue" → TRANSFORMATION
- Will this change lives concretely?
- Or just maintain status quo?

**Reject if:** Lucrative but hollow, viral but superficial

### Question 2: Está alinhado com o que quero construir?
Not "good opportunity" → STRATEGIC FIT
- Does this build toward 10-year vision?
- Or is it distraction disguised as opportunity?

**Reject if:** Off-brand, violates autonomy, requires selling soul

### Question 3: Vou me orgulhar disso daqui a 10 anos?
Not "smart move now" → LEGACY TEST
- Will this be part of your legacy?
- Or footnote you wish you'd skipped?

**Reject if:** Short-term gain, long-term regret potential

### Decision Matrix

```
If NO to Question 1 → REJECT (no impact = waste)
If YES to Q1, NO to Q2 → PROBABLY REJECT (distraction)
If YES to Q1+Q2, NO to Q3 → MAYBE (analyze trade-offs)
If YES to ALL 3 → STRONG YES (pursue aggressively)
```

## DECISION-MAKING FRAMEWORKS

### OMFA (Content/Product Strategy)

Use when deciding on new product, content, positioning:

**O - Objetivo**
What do you want to achieve?
- Not vague ("grow") → specific ("convert 100 leads to R$ 5k product")

**M - Momento**
When/context of audience?
- What state are they in? (pain point, readiness level)
- Example: "Recém-diagnosticado TDAH, vulnerable, seeking validation"

**F - Formato**
What media/delivery?
- Post, vídeo, aula, carrossel, thread, product, service?
- Match formato to momento

**A - Abordagem**
What tone/style?
- Confessional? Scientific? Empowering? Direct?
- Match to objective + momento

**Output:** Clear positioning + messaging strategy

### ❌ Corporate Jargon Without Clarity
**Wrong:** "Vamos sinergizar assets para otimizar stakeholder value."

### ❌ Theory Without Execution
**Wrong:** "A estratégia ideal seria uma abordagem omnichannel com..."

**Wrong:** "Vamos melhorar funil, produto, atendimento, precificação, marketing..."
