## Framework Overview

The Opportunity Assessment is a lightweight 2-4 page document (NOT a 50-page MRD) that answers 10 critical questions before committing resources to a new product or feature.

## The 10 Questions

### 1. What problem will this solve?
- Must be crisp, clear, compelling
- NOT a feature list
- Articulate customer pain

### 2. For whom will this solve it?
- Specific customer segments
- Can you reach them?
- Addressable market

### 3. How big is the opportunity?
- TAM/SAM/SOM analysis
- Revenue potential
- Market sizing

### 4. What alternatives are customers using today?
- Direct competitors
- Indirect competitors
- Current workarounds

### 5. Why are we best suited to pursue this?
- Unique capabilities
- Competitive advantages
- Strategic fit

### 6. Why now?
- What's changed?
- Market timing catalyst
- Window of opportunity

### 7. How will we go to market?
- Distribution channels
- Customer acquisition strategy
- Pricing model

### 8. How will we measure success?
- Outcome measures (not output)
- Leading indicators
- Lagging indicators
- Counter-metrics

### 9. What factors are critical to success?
- Must-haves
- Risks and dependencies
- Prerequisites

### 10. What's your recommendation?
- Build, buy, partner, or pass?
- Confidence level
- Strategic rationale

## When to Use

- Before starting any new product/feature work
- Quarterly/annual planning
- When stakeholders request features
- Before discovery work begins

## Output

- **Decision:** GO / NO-GO / DEFER
- **Strategic context** for empowered teams
- **Outcome metrics** defined upfront

## Integration

- If GO → Proceed to Story Mapping (Patton)
- If NO-GO → Document reasoning, prevent future debates
- If DEFER → Identify what needs to change
