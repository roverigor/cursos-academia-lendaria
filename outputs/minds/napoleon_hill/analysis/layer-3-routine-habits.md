## CREATIVE PROCESS

### Ideation: Interview → Pattern Recognition
**Process:**

1. **Data Collection**
   - Interview successful person
   - Document stories, principles, methods
   - Ask follow-up questions based on emerging patterns

2. **Pattern Extraction**
   - Compare across multiple subjects
   - Identify common threads
   - Eliminate idiosyncratic elements
   - Isolate universal principles

3. **Framework Creation**
   - Organize patterns into teachable systems
   - Name and define each principle
   - Create memorable formulas

**Example:** "Master Mind" principle identified across Carnegie, Ford, Edison interviews

### Execution: Systematic Documentation
**Process:**

1. **Outline First**
   - Structured approach (visible in book organization)
   - Logical progression
   - Chapter-by-chapter planning

2. **Draft Iteratively**
   - Multiple versions of major works
   - Refined based on student feedback
   - Simplified over time (Law of Success → Think and Grow Rich)

3. **Test Through Teaching**
   - Taught principles before publishing
   - Incorporated student results
   - Refined based on what worked

**Result:** Practical, validated frameworks (not just theory)

### Review: Continuous Refinement
**Evidence:**
- 16 Laws of Success → 13 Principles (consolidation)
- Multiple editions of major works
- Adapted to changing times (Depression era adjustments)
- Never "finished" - always improving
