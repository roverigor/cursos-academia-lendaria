## Conversion Funnel Frameworks

### 1. TOFU/MOFU/BOFU (Top/Middle/Bottom of Funnel)

**TOFU (Awareness):**
- Goal: Attract attention, build awareness
- Content: Blog posts, social media, videos, podcasts
- Metrics: Impressions, reach, traffic
- CTA: "Learn more", "Subscribe", "Download guide"

**MOFU (Consideration):**
- Goal: Build trust, demonstrate value
- Content: Case studies, webinars, email courses, comparisons
- Metrics: Engagement, email opens, time on site
- CTA: "Sign up for trial", "Book demo", "Join webinar"

**BOFU (Decision):**
- Goal: Convert to customer
- Content: Free trials, consultations, testimonials, ROI calculators
- Metrics: Conversions, MRR, CAC
- CTA: "Buy now", "Start trial", "Get quote"

**Usage in CreatorOS:**
- Map content pieces to funnel stages
- Track performance by stage
- Optimize conversion rates between stages

---

### 2. Pirate Metrics (AARRR)

**Framework:** Dave McClure's startup growth funnel

**Acquisition:**
- Where do users come from?
- Channels: SEO, social, paid ads, referrals

**Activation:**
- Do they have a great first experience?
- Metric: "Aha moment" completion

**Retention:**
- Do they come back?
- Metric: DAU/MAU, churn rate

**Referral:**
- Do they tell others?
- Metric: Viral coefficient, NPS

**Revenue:**
- Do they pay?
- Metric: LTV, ARPU, CAC

**Usage in CreatorOS:**
- Track content performance across AARRR
- Identify drop-off points
- Optimize each stage independently

---

### 3. Jobs to Be Done (JTBD)

**Core Concept:** People "hire" products to do a job

**Framework:**
- Functional job (what needs to get done?)
- Emotional job (how do they want to feel?)
- Social job (how do they want to be seen?)

**Example (CreatorOS):**
- Functional: "I need to create 10 blog posts/month"
- Emotional: "I want to feel confident my voice is preserved"
- Social: "I want to be seen as a prolific thought leader"

**Usage in CreatorOS:**
- Write content addressing all 3 dimensions
- Position product around jobs, not features
- Use JTBD language in copy

### 4. Value Ladder

**Purpose:** Progressively increase customer value and price

**Structure:**
1. Free (Lead magnet) - Newsletter, ebook, checklist
2. Tripwire ($7-47) - Mini course, template pack
3. Core Offer ($97-497) - Main product/course
4. Premium ($997-2997) - Coaching, certification
5. VIP ($5K+) - Done-for-you, consulting

### 5. Hook, Story, Offer (Russell Brunson)

**Structure:** Persuasion sequence

**Hook:**
- Grab attention (stat, question, bold claim)
- Pattern interrupt
- Promise transformation

**Story:**
- Relatability (I was like you)
- Journey (struggle, discovery, breakthrough)
- Proof (results, testimonials)

**Offer:**
- Solution (what you get)
- Stack (value components)
- Guarantee (risk reversal)
- Urgency (scarcity, bonuses)

**Usage in CreatorOS:**
- Structure sales pages
- Write email sequences
- Create webinar scripts

## Content Strategy Frameworks

### 6. Hub and Spoke Model

**Structure:** Central pillar content + supporting pieces

**Hub (Pillar):**
- Comprehensive guide (3000+ words)
- Video course
- Podcast series

**Spokes (Supporting):**
- Blog posts on sub-topics
- Social posts highlighting key points
- Email series expanding concepts
- Infographics summarizing

**Usage in CreatorOS:**
- Plan content clusters
- Link spokes to hub (SEO benefit)
- Repurpose efficiently

---

### 7. Content Marketing Matrix (Smart Insights)

**Quadrants:**

**1. Entertain (Emotional + Awareness):**
- Viral content, memes, stories
- Goal: Reach, shareability

**2. Inspire (Emotional + Buy):**
- Case studies, testimonials, visions
- Goal: Desire, aspiration

**3. Educate (Rational + Awareness):**
- How-to, guides, tutorials
- Goal: Authority, trust

**4. Convince (Rational + Buy):**
- Comparisons, ROI, specs
- Goal: Conversion, logic

**Usage in CreatorOS:**
- Balance content across quadrants
- Match quadrant to funnel stage
- Track performance by quadrant

---

### 8. E-E-A-T (Google Quality Guidelines)

**Framework:** Content quality assessment

**Experience:**
- First-hand experience with topic
- Real-world examples

**Expertise:**
- Credentials, knowledge depth
- Accuracy, thoroughness

**Authoritativeness:**
- Recognized as go-to source
- Citations, mentions

**Trust:**
- Transparent, honest
- Updated, maintained

**Usage in CreatorOS:**
- Emphasize instructor experience
- Showcase credentials
- Collect testimonials (social proof)
- Update content regularly

## Attribution Models

### 9. Multi-Touch Attribution

**Models:**

**First-Touch:**
- Credit first interaction
- Best for: Measuring awareness channels

**Last-Touch:**
- Credit final interaction
- Best for: Measuring conversion drivers

**Linear:**
- Equal credit to all touches
- Best for: Long consideration cycles

**Time Decay:**
- More credit to recent touches
- Best for: Sales-driven orgs

**Position-Based (U-Shaped):**
- 40% first, 40% last, 20% middle
- Best for: Balanced view

**Usage in CreatorOS:**
- Track content touchpoints
- Assign revenue to content pieces
- Optimize high-impact content

### 10. Bullseye Framework (Traction)

**Purpose:** Find best marketing channels

**Process:**
1. **Outer Ring:** Brainstorm all possible channels (19 standard)
2. **Middle Ring:** Test top 3-5 with cheap experiments
3. **Inner Ring:** Double down on #1 winner

**19 Channels:**
- Targeting blogs
- Publicity
- Unconventional PR
- SEM
- Social ads
- Offline ads
- SEO
- Content marketing
- Email marketing
- Viral marketing
- Engineering as marketing
- Business development
- Sales
- Affiliate programs
- Existing platforms
- Trade shows
- Offline events
- Speaking engagements
- Community building

**Usage in CreatorOS:**
- Test content distribution channels
- Measure CAC per channel
- Focus on highest ROI channels
